  <p style="margin-top:1em" class="description">
    There are a lot of videos here. If you&#8217;re only planning to write apps for the market, you&#8217;ll most likely not be interested in the Embedded Android talk or Scala as a Java replacement. If you don&#8217;t work on iOS and never plan to, you can probably scan through Nick Farina&#8217;s talk (sorry, Nick). I found many of the videos worthwhile, whether they related to actual app development or the business of apps.<br xmlns:pr="xalan://com.pufferfish.core.beans.xmlbuilders.xsl.Functions" /><br />I also really enjoyed the keynotes. Tim O&#8217;Reilly&#8217;s talk is probably my favorite because I&#8217;ve been saying the same things about many of Tim&#8217;s comparisons since I bought my first DROID a couple of years ago.
  </p>
  
  <p>
    Even if you&#8217;re familiar with Android development, you should definitely watch the videos by Ken Jones. He shows the start of a Twitter client. Although he didn&#8217;t get very far in the actual coding, he will point you to the finished code. One aspect I loved about his talks is that he discusses a lot of best practices. Many tutorials you find online either don&#8217;t discuss best practices or don&#8217;t incorporate them.
  </p>
  
  <p>
    Be sure to watch the Arduino videos. I hadn&#8217;t heard about Arduino before this video and I was very impressed. In fact, I watched the keynote video twice.
  </p>
  
  <p>
    Honestly, there are some videos here that I&#8217;m not that interested in. That doesn&#8217;t detract from the overall usefulness of this video collection. If you&#8217;re 25% of the way through a video and it hasn&#8217;t hooked you yet, move onto the next because there is a plethora of information.
  </p>
